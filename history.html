History of Last Bacates

In late 2023, the Middleridge Cakes was a group known for their relentless ding-dong ditching and creative base-building in Middleridge Park. Their activities ranged from simple pranks to creating elaborate hiding spots.

After the disbandment of Middleridge Cakes, the All Day Bacates emerged with ten members. They escalated their activities, building a sophisticated tunnel system extending up to 80 feet. Unfortunately, the tunnels were destroyed during a severe flood, leading to a series of dramatic events.

One significant incident occurred during a confrontation with the Third Rivalry faction. The All Day Bacates found themselves trapped in a tunnel with stale air and little oxygen. To escape, they played a fake police siren sound effect on their phone, scaring off the Third Rivalry members and managing to escape just in time before their situation became critical.

Another notable incident involved being surrounded by rival factions during a flood. The All Day Bacates members had to hide while the creek was flooded, and some were trapped due to quicksand and enemy factions. This situation, while dangerous, was more of a play fight, highlighting the group’s resilience and resourcefulness.

Cool Things

  • The complex tunnel systems built by the All Day Bacates were so intricate that even experts struggled to map them fully.
  • The use of sound effects and psychological tactics to escape dangerous situations demonstrates the group's creativity and quick thinking.
  • Despite their controversial activities, the group has maintained a strong sense of camaraderie and loyalty.
  • Many of the group's hidden bases remain undisclosed, adding to their mystique and allure.
